Our Story

Born from clinical need. Built for clinical use.

PhenoTips began in 2012 as a joint research project between computer scientists at the University of Toronto and geneticists at SickKids Hospital — and was the first clinical tool to operationalize the Human Phenotype Ontology. Today it serves genetics programs at 100+ institutions across North America, Europe, and beyond.

Mission

End the diagnostic odyssey for every patient.

The average rare disease patient waits 4 to 7 years for a diagnosis. They see specialist after specialist, their history re-entered from scratch at each visit, their phenotype data scattered across disconnected systems.

PhenoTips exists to close that gap — through structured data, connected workflows, and tools that clinicians actually want to use.

History

Built incrementally, alongside clinicians.

Every feature in PhenoTips was shaped by the genetics programs that use it — starting from a single research collaboration between the University of Toronto and SickKids, growing into a platform trusted by programs on four continents.

2012

Began as a joint research project between computer scientists at the University of Toronto and geneticists at SickKids Hospital — building the first clinical tool to operationalize the Human Phenotype Ontology (HPO).

2014

Incorporated as a clinical software company. Began rolling out PhenoTips to academic medical centers and rare disease programs across North America.

2017

Cancer risk assessment module launched. CanRisk, Gail, Tyrer-Cuzick, PREMM5, and IBIS integrated into a single pedigree workflow.

2019

SMART on FHIR–based EHR embedding launched for Epic and Cerner. DSPT and CE+ certification achieved for UK healthcare deployments.

2021

Clinical Cloud launched. A lightweight, purpose-built version of PhenoTips for individual counselors and smaller clinical teams — same workflow, no enterprise integration required.

2024

100+ clinical institutions. HIPAA, GDPR, DSPT, SOC2, and CE+ certified. AI-assisted test recommendation rolled into the platform. Named to Canada's 200 Best Startup Employers.

Why it matters

The problem has always been data.

Clinical genomics generates extraordinary complexity — family histories spanning three generations, phenotype data captured in free text, variant findings living in lab systems disconnected from the clinical record, risk models housed in separate tools that require the same data entered multiple times.

PhenoTips was designed to solve this at the data layer — by making genomic information structured, interoperable, and actionable at the point of care.

That is why it was built at a hospital, not a startup. And why it has stayed in continuous clinical use for over a decade.
